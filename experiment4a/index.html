Experiment 4a - Offset Brush

- click or drag the mouse

Description

Offset Brush is an infinite procedural world focused on building dynamic terrain and structures. Players can click or drag the mouse to batch-build rooftops and floor tiles, while the ground continuously fluctuates over time to create a lively environment.

Technical

- Implemented a procedurally generated tile-based map.

- Added mouse click and drag batch-building functionality to activate multiple tiles simultaneously.

- Introduced a wave effect that makes the ground and buildings rise and fall rhythmically over time.

- Used seed control (key) to ensure world reproducibility and variation.

Reflection

This project helped me understand the basics of procedural generation and interactive systems. Although the initial version felt repetitive, introducing batch-building and dynamic terrain greatly improved the experience. One major challenge was resolving tile overlap issues during construction, which I eventually solved by adding placement detection logic..
